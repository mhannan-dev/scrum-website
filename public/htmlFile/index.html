Six Sigma and Lean Six Sigma Training

Six Sigma & Lean Six Sigma

  • Six Sigma is a structured, data-driven methodology focused on improving business processes by reducing variation and eliminating defects. It combines statistical tools with project management principles to achieve measurable results in quality, efficiency, and customer satisfaction.
  • Lean Six Sigma integrates Lean principles (which focus on eliminating waste and improving flow) into the traditional Six Sigma framework.

Why the Confusion?

  • The military popularized the term "Lean Six Sigma" when they adopted Six Sigma but incorporated Lean thinking to better suit operational efficiency goals.
  • As a result, many assume they're fundamentally different programs, but in reality, most modern Six Sigma courses already include Lean concepts.

Core Principles

  1. Customer Focus: Define success from the customer's perspective. Identify Critical to Quality (CTQ) characteristics based on the Voice of the Customer (VOC).
  2. Data-Driven Decision Making: Use statistical methods to guide decisions, analyze problems, and validate improvements
  3. Process Improvement: Improve consistency and efficiency by eliminating waste, defects, and variability in processes.
  4. Employee Involvement: Engage all organizational levels through structured roles (White, Yellow, Green, Black, and Master Black Belts).
  5. Sustainable Results: Ensure improvements are maintained through standardization, control plans, and continuous monitoring.

Aligned with the Council for Six Sigma Certification (CSSC) standards, this course is ideal for individuals who support process improvement projects under the direction of Green or Black Belts. Yellow Belts are instrumental in identifying process inefficiencies, collecting data, and applying basic tools to support quality improvement efforts.

Green Belt Certification

Program Overview: The Lean Six Sigma Green Belt Certification equips professionals with the knowledge and tools to lead process improvement initiatives within their teams or departments. Aligned with the Council for Six Sigma Certification (CSSC) standards, this program emphasizes the DMAIC methodology, data analysis, and practical application of Lean principles. Green Belts operate under the guidance of Black Belts and serve as project leaders on moderate-scale improvement efforts.

Course Objectives

  • ‚úÖUnderstand and apply the DMAIC framework in real-world projects
  • ‚úÖAnalyze process data to identify root causes of variation and waste
  • ‚úÖ Use Lean and Six Sigma tools to improve quality and efficiency
  • ‚úÖLead Green Belt-level projects and support Black Belt initiatives
  • ‚úÖ Communicate project findings using control charts and process metrics

Course Modules

  1. Introduction to Lean Six Sigma
    • History and benefits of Lean Six Sigma
    • Overview of Lean principles and Six Sigma methodology
    • Roles and responsibilities across Belt levels
  2. Define Phase
    • Project selection and scoping
    • Developing project charters
    • Voice of the Customer (VOC) and Critical to Quality (CTQ)
    • High-level process mapping (SIPOC)
  3. Measure Phase
    • Process flowcharting and detailed mapping
    • Data collection planning and tools
    • Descriptive statistics (mean, median, mode, standard deviation)
    • Measurement System Analysis (MSA)
    • Process capability analysis (Cp, Cpk, Pp, Ppk)
  4. Analyze Phase
    • Root cause analysis: Fishbone diagrams, 5 Whys
    • Graphical analysis tools: Histograms, Pareto, Boxplots, Scatterplots
    • Hypothesis testing (t-tests, chi-square, ANOVA)
    • Correlation and regression analysis
  5. Improve Phase
    • Brainstorming and idea generation
    • Solution selection matrices (e.g., Pugh matrix)
    • Piloting and implementation of improvements
    • Lean tools: 5S, SMED, mistake-proofing (Poka-Yoke), Kaizen
  6. Control Phase
    • Control charting for variables and attributes (Xbar-R, I-MR, P, U charts)
    • Developing and executing control plans
    • Documenting Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)
    • Transitioning projects to process owners

Black Belt Certification

Program Overview: The Lean Six Sigma Black Belt Certification is designed for professionals who are ready to lead complex, cross-functional process improvement projects. Aligned with the Council for Six Sigma Certification (CSSC) Body of Knowledge, this program develops advanced problem-solving, data analysis, and leadership capabilities to drive strategic improvement initiatives across the enterprise. Black Belts are responsible for mentoring Green Belts, managing large-scale projects, and delivering high-impact organizational change.

Course Objectives

  • ‚úÖ Master the application of advanced Lean Six Sigma tools and methods
  • ‚úÖ Lead high-level process improvement projects using the DMAIC methodology
  • ‚úÖ Conduct detailed statistical analysis to drive fact-based decisions
  • ‚úÖ Facilitate team dynamics, stakeholder engagement, and change leadership
  • ‚úÖ Align process improvements with business strategy and KPIs

Course Modules

  1. Enterprise-Wide Deployment
    • Lean Six Sigma strategy and infrastructure
    • Project portfolio management and selection
    • Organizational metrics and value stream alignment
  2. Define Phase
    • Project charter and stakeholder analysis
    • Voice of the Customer (VOC) and Critical to Quality (CTQ) identification
    • High-level process mapping (SIPOC)
    • Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ)
  3. Measure Phase
    • Measurement system analysis (Gage R&R, bias, linearity)
    • Process capability for normal and non-normal data (Cp, Cpk, Pp, Ppk)
    • Sampling plans and data collection methods
    • Baseline performance metrics and defect measurement
  4. Analyze Phase
    • Root cause analysis: Multi-vari, Fishbone, 5 Whys
    • Hypothesis testing: t-tests, ANOVA, Chi-square, proportion tests
    • Regression analysis and correlation
    • Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)
  5. Improve Phase
    • Design of Experiments (DOE): Full factorial, fractional factorial, response surface methodology
    • Optimization techniques (EVOP, Taguchi)
    • Lean tools: Kaizen, Poka-Yoke, line balancing, SMED
    • Pilot testing and implementation planning
  6. Control Phase
    • Control charts for variables and attributes (Xbar-R, I-MR, P, C, U, NP)
    • Control plans and response strategies
    • Standard work documentation (SOPs)
    • Project closure and sustainment practices
